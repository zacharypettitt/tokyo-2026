Where we'd send you to eat

Food

Every meal worth repeating — and the one we'd steer you away from. Ordering was easier than we feared; touchscreen kiosks and picture menus carried us through. Filter by area below.

Shibuya

Uobei Shibuya Dogenzaka ★

Our favorite meal of the trip, full stop. Conveyor-belt sushi ordered from a touchscreen, delivered on a little track to your seat — fast, genuinely delicious, and so affordable we went back three times. If you eat one thing on this list, make it this.

Shibuya

Starbucks (in the station)

Not a "specialty" pick, but our unsung hero of the trip. Tucked just outside our hotel's entrance, still inside the main metro station, it gave us a quick first breakfast every single morning before heading out — fast calories before the day's adventures, with a proper second breakfast once we'd gotten going. Incredibly convenient; we leaned on it daily.

Shibuya

Super Oreryu Shio-Ramen

Our first meal in Japan, and the gentlest possible introduction: order at a touchscreen kiosk with an English menu, pay by card, sit down to a great bowl of shio ramen. We came back the very next day.

Ueno

Ueno Park festival carts

We lucked into a festival in Ueno Park and made dinner out of the food carts — grilled skewers, street snacks, the works. If there's an event on while you're there, graze your way through it.

Only there during festivals — check what's on before you count on it.

Estacio Cafe (hotel breakfast)

The breakfast spot at our hotel — convenient and calm on the mornings we didn't feel like hunting, with a surprisingly great view down over Shibuya Crossing. Handy to know it's there when you want an easy start.

We'll be straight with you: this one didn't work for us. We ended up in the upstairs section where smoking was permitted, which wasn't pleasant. Plenty of great spots nearby — we'd point you elsewhere.
