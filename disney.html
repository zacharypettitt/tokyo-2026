One long, wonderful day

Tokyo Disney

We did DisneySea and Disneyland in a single day and came away with clear advice. Here's the verdict, the prep, the surprisingly tricky bus, and the strategy that made the day work.

Do
DisneySea.

If you only have one day, spend it all at DisneySea. It's full of rides and areas we'd never seen anywhere else. Disneyland, by contrast, felt like a smaller, Japanese-language copy of Anaheim — and the Jungle Cruise jokes just don't land when you can't follow them. We split our day across both and wished we'd committed to DisneySea.

01

Before you go

  • Buy tickets in advance. Don't leave this to the gate.
  • Download the app. Everything you'll need on the day lives in it — ride reservations, wait times, the map.
  • Link your party in the app. Once everyone's linked, one person can book ride reservations for the whole group, which saves a lot of scrambling.
02

Getting there: the Shinjuku bus

We took the dedicated Disney bus from Shinjuku, which is lovely once you're on it — no train transfers to worry about — but genuinely tricky to find the first time. Here's the path:

  1. Take the Yamanote Line into Shinjuku, then exit the station and cross the street to the Shinjuku Expressway Bus Terminal.
  2. Go up to level 4F to buy your bus tickets.
  3. Then walk around to the far side of the parking lot, where the Disney buses actually depart.

03

Day-of strategy

Good to know 豆知識

Arrive hours before opening. We got there at 7:15 for a 9:00 open and were still well back in a huge line — but it didn't wreck the day or keep us off rides. Just don't expect to stroll in at opening.

Once the park fills, standby lines balloon to two or three hours. You can pay roughly $10–15 per person, per ride to skip big chunks of the line — and with only one day, that was clearly worth it. Treat it as part of the ticket cost, not an extra.

Our rhythm: as soon as you're in, beeline to one headliner (we ran to Journey to the Center of the Earth) and, while you're heading there, book a FastPass for your next ride. You can only hold one FastPass at a time, so pick a return window that opens right when you finish the first ride — then chain from one to the next all day.

04

Don't miss: Mermaid Lagoon

If you're at DisneySea, find Mermaid Lagoon. It's a huge indoor cavern area right in the middle of the park that's weirdly easy to walk straight past. Because it's fully indoors, it's a perfect break from sun or rain, it's packed with gentler rides for younger kids, and the restaurant tucked inside — crowded but pleasant — lets you eat "indoors" in the middle of the day.
